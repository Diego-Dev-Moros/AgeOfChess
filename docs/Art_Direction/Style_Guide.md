# Style Guide

---

# 1. Overall Artistic Direction

Age of Chess uses a magical medieval pixel art style built around chess statues, ancient board arenas, dice-based combat, and readable tactical play.

The target style is a SNES-inspired pixel art foundation with modern readability:

- Crisp silhouettes.
- Limited but rich palettes.
- Strong top-left lighting.
- High-value highlights used sparingly.
- Decorative fantasy details concentrated in cards, frames, and reward moments.

The game should feel ancient, tactical, and arcane. It should not feel cartoonish, soft, painterly, or overly dark.

---

# 2. Visual Tone and Atmosphere

The core tone is:

- Strategic.
- Magical.
- Medieval.
- Slightly ominous.
- Heroic only when a card or combat effect calls for it.

The world should suggest that chess is a ritual battlefield. Pieces are not ordinary plastic or wooden tokens; they are carved, enchanted objects used in a formal magical conflict.

---

# 3. Inspirations and References

Primary reference:

- The provided Giant Slayer card: ornate frame, parchment nameplate, dark magical illustration field, glowing chess pieces, dice symbols, and green-gold buff language.

Supporting inspiration categories:

- SNES-era tactical RPG sprites.
- Pixel fantasy card frames.
- Medieval illuminated manuscript borders.
- Stone chess sets.
- Arcane runes and carved dice.

Do not copy reference images literally. Extract the structure:

- Thick readable frame.
- Clear title hierarchy.
- Central focal object.
- Controlled particles.
- Limited color identity per type.

---

# 4. Clarity vs Detail Balance

Gameplay readability wins over decorative density.

| Asset | Detail Budget | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cards | High | Detail is allowed because cards are inspected outside the board grid |
| Pieces | Medium | Silhouette must read before material detail |
| Board | Low to medium | Tile separation must remain clear at all times |
| UI | Low | Information must be faster to read than decoration |
| Dice | Medium | Pips/faces must be immediately legible |

If an effect, ornament, or texture makes a piece, tile, card type, HP value, or dice result harder to read, remove it.

---

# 5. Production Rule

Every new visual asset must pass three checks:

1. It reads at gameplay size.
2. It uses the shared palette and lighting direction.
3. It belongs to the same magical chess world.
