# Immoral

---

# 1. Card Type

Debuff

---

# 2. Category

Offensive

---

# 3. Effect

Enemy pieces attack using their Defense Dice value instead of their Attack Dice value.

Example:

```txt
Rook normally:
AD = 4
DD = 3

With Immoral:
Rook attacks with 3 dice.
```

---

# 4. Trigger

Whenever an affected enemy piece attacks in normal combat.

---

# 5. Target

All enemy non-King pieces.

---

# 6. Duration

Entire match.

---

# 7. Restrictions

- Does not affect Defense Dice.
- Does not affect the King.
- Does not apply during Frenzy.
- Top 3 Dice Rule still applies.
- Cannot reduce a piece below 1 attack die.

---

# 8. Design Intent

Immoral weakens offensive consistency.

It is designed to slow down aggressive strategies and force the affected player to rely more on positioning than raw attacks.

---

# 9. Strategic Use

This Debuff encourages:

- Defensive setups
- Punishing overextension
- Reducing enemy attack reliability
- Forcing the opponent into slower play

---

# 10. Counterplay

The affected player can respond by:

- Using pieces with naturally strong Defense Dice
- Waiting for better attack positions
- Using the King’s Divine Attack
- Playing for Frenzy
- Creating check threats instead of direct combat trades

---

# 11. Example

```txt
White Queen attacks Black Rook.

White Queen normally:
AD = 4
DD = 3

White is affected by Immoral.

White Queen attacks with 3 dice instead of 4.
```

---

# 12. Balance Notes

Risk:

- Can make attacking feel weak if combined with defensive terrain.

Controls:

- Does not affect the King.
- Does not apply during Frenzy.
- Cannot reduce attack dice below 1.
- Top 3 Dice Rule keeps the effect bounded.
