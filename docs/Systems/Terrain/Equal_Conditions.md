# Equal Conditions

---

# 1. Terrain Type

Equalizing

---

# 2. Effect

During combat, the dice difference between attacker and defender is capped at:

```txt
1 die
```

This means one side cannot use more than 1 additional die over the other side during a combat comparison.

---

# 3. Trigger

Triggers whenever normal combat begins.

---

# 4. Affected Pieces

All non-King pieces from both players.

---

# 5. Duration

Entire match.

The effect stops applying during Frenzy Phase.

---

# 6. Restrictions

- Does not affect the King.
- Does not apply during Frenzy.
- Does not change base AD or DD permanently.
- Only limits usable dice during combat.
- Top 3 Dice Rule still applies.
- Does not affect HP.
- Does not affect damage directly.

---

# 7. Design Intent

Equal Conditions exists to reduce the gap between weaker and stronger pieces.

It makes the battlefield more volatile and gives low-value pieces a better chance to survive or threaten stronger pieces.

The goal is not to make all pieces equal, but to reduce extreme dice advantages.

---

# 8. Strategic Impact

This Terrain encourages:

- More aggressive pawn and minor-piece attacks
- Riskier trades
- Less reliance on Queen dominance
- Greater importance of positioning
- More tactical uncertainty

Strong pieces remain valuable because of:

- Higher HP
- Better movement
- Better positioning flexibility
- Stronger base stats

But their dice advantage is reduced.

---

# 9. Counterplay

Players can adapt by:

- Using high-HP pieces to absorb trades
- Relying more on positioning than raw dice advantage
- Creating multiple threats instead of single strong attacks
- Preserving pieces for Frenzy
- Using Buffs that increase damage rather than dice count

---

# 10. Example

```txt
Queen attacks Pawn.

Queen AD = 4
Pawn DD = 1

Normal difference:
4 - 1 = 3 dice

Equal Conditions caps the difference to 1.

The Queen may only use 2 dice against the Pawn's 1 defense die.
```

---

# 11. Interaction with Top 3 Dice Rule

The Top 3 Dice Rule still applies after Equal Conditions.

Example:

```txt
Rook attacks Bishop.

Rook AD = 4
Bishop DD = 2

Equal Conditions caps Rook usable dice to 3.

Top 3 Dice Rule also allows max 3.

Rook uses 3 dice.
```

---

# 12. Interaction with Buffs

Equal Conditions may reduce the value of dice-increasing Buffs.

However, damage-based Buffs still apply normally.

Example:

- Giant Slayer can still add damage.
- Stone Pieces can still raise minimum damage.
- Iron Age may still improve defense but remains subject to the cap.

---

# 13. Interaction with Debuffs

Equal Conditions may soften or amplify Debuffs depending on the case.

Examples:

- Immoral may matter less if dice differences are already capped.
- Cowardice remains fully relevant because it affects reliability, not dice count.

---

# 14. Interaction with Frenzy

During Frenzy:

- Dice are disabled.
- Equal Conditions no longer applies.

---

# 15. Balance Notes

Risk:

- May make high-value pieces feel less powerful.

Controls:

- HP and movement still preserve piece identity.
- The cap affects only dice usage.
- Damage bonuses and board control remain important.
